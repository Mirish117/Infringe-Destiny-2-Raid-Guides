Team Tip

Divinity puzzle runs are slower than normal clears. Assign a puzzle lead before starting and make sure nobody rushes into the next encounter before the puzzle step is confirmed.

1

Embrace

The opening encounter introduces tethering and relay movement. The Consecrated Mind drops Voltaic Overflow, and teams must rotate correctly while opening doors and defending relays.

Embrace encounter
Embrace guide

Roles

Boss Team

Stays with the Consecrated Mind, picks up Voltaic Overflow, and keeps the boss moving forward.

Door Team

Moves ahead, clears enemies, and uses tether boxes to open the next section.

Mechanics

  1. Split into two teams of three.
  2. One team stays near the boss and manages Voltaic Overflow pickups.
  3. The other team pushes forward to clear adds and open doors with tethers.
  4. Rotate teams when needed so Voltaic Overflow does not cause deaths.
  5. Continue the chase until the final sprint opens.
2

Summon the Consecrated Mind

This encounter centers around defending relays, collecting motes, tethering boxes, and stopping Vex sacrifices. Good lane control makes the encounter much easier.

Summon the Consecrated Mind encounter

Roles

Relay Defenders

Protect active relays and kill Vex before they sacrifice.

Floaters

Move between relays, support weak sides, and help complete tether links.

Mechanics

  1. Split players across the relays.
  2. Defend relays from Vex sacrifices.
  3. Use tether boxes to link and activate each relay.
  4. Rotate floaters to whichever relay is under pressure.
  5. Complete the final tether sequence to finish the encounter.
Important

Do not let enemies sacrifice at a relay. Sacrifices quickly create instability and can wipe the team.

Baseball / Relay Puzzle

This section uses tether positioning and player spacing. The guide image gives the cleanest visual for where players should stand.

Baseball guide

Mechanics

  1. Assign players to fixed positions before starting the tether.
  2. Make sure each player is spaced correctly so the tether chain connects.
  3. Move only when the puzzle lead calls for it.
  4. Confirm the puzzle completes before moving into the next section.

5

Consecrated Mind

Consecrated Mind is a boss encounter with eyes, motes, relays, and a chase-style damage phase. Players need clean calls and fast mote deposits.

Consecrated Mind encounter
Consecrated Mind guide

Roles

Eye Team

Reads and shoots the correct inner or outer eyes when the boss opens.

Mote Team

Enters portals, kills enemies, collects motes, and banks at the relay.

Defenders

Protect relays, kill Cyclops, and stop Vex sacrifices.

Mechanics

  1. Split into eye team, mote team, and defenders.
  2. Boss opens its eyes. Call inner or outer and shoot only the correct eyes.
  3. Mote players enter portals, collect motes, and deposit them at the active relay.
  4. Defenders stop Vex sacrifices and kill Cyclops quickly.
  5. When the boss runs, follow and damage the exposed weak point.
  6. Repeat until Consecrated Mind is defeated.
Challenge Mode

To the Top: always deposit ten motes at a time. Do not bank partial mote amounts.

6

Sanctified Mind

Sanctified Mind is the final boss. The encounter uses portals, motes, relay deposits, platform rebuilding, tethering, and boss damage phases.

Sanctified Mind encounter
Sanctified Mind guide

Roles

Portal Teams

Enter blue/orange portals, collect motes, and bank them at the correct relay.

Build Team

Rebuilds missing platforms using tether boxes when the arena is damaged.

Tether Team

Links the correct relay to the boss to open the damage phase.

Mechanics

  1. Shoot the boss weak point to open the correct portal.
  2. Portal teams enter, collect motes, and return to bank at the matching relay.
  3. Defenders stop Vex sacrifices and control Cyclops spawns.
  4. Build team restores missing platforms when safe.
  5. Once the relay is charged, tether the correct relay to the boss.
  6. Group for damage and repeat until the boss dies.
Challenge Mode

Zero to One Hundred: fully charge a relay quickly after the first mote deposit. Plan portal rotations before starting.
