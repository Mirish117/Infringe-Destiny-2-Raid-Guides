Predestination / Raid Hub

Desert Perpetual uses a non-linear flow for the first three bosses. After the opening section, the fireteam can choose which boss route to take before returning to the central structure and selecting the next encounter.

Boss Order

The first three boss encounters can be completed in any order. Once Agarios, Iatros, and Epoptes are cleared, the raid progresses into the final encounter against Keregos.

Basic Hub Flow

  1. Clear enemies in the central raid space.
  2. Interact with the chosen route object to select a boss path.
  3. Complete the selected encounter.
  4. Return to the hub and choose the next remaining boss.
  5. After all three are complete, move into Keregos.
1

Agarios, Inherent

Agarios is the Hobgoblin-style boss encounter. The team manages Chronons, portals, Temporality alignment, sniper-line-of-sight pressure, and damage extensions.

Agarios encounter guide

Roles

Plate Players

Stand on assigned plates, receive Temporality buffs, and start alignment with the matching boss form.

Chronon Team

Collects Chronons and banks them into the correct active portals.

Aligned Player

Runs the portal chain, gains full alignment, then uses the grenade input to deflect the sniper shot.

Mechanics

  1. Clear adds until Chronons begin dropping.
  2. Use Chronons to activate portals around the arena.
  3. Plate players stand on plates and identify their Temporality buff.
  4. Interact with the boss form that matches the active buff.
  5. Call the inactive portals so the Chronon team can bank into them.
  6. Aligned player runs through portals to complete alignment.
  7. Break line of sight from the sniper shot, then deflect it to start or extend damage.
  8. Damage Agarios and repeat until defeated.
Damage Extension

During damage, one player should prepare the next full alignment so the team can deflect another sniper shot and extend DPS.

2

Iatros, Inward-Turned

Iatros is the Wyvern-style encounter. The team banks Chronons through the hoop, uses metronome timing, shoots pillar nodes, creates climbing platforms, and reaches a damage phase.

Iatros encounter guide

Roles

Pillar Players

Watch assigned pillars and shoot the correct nodes on timing.

Runner / Climber

Uses spawned platforms to climb and interact with the objective.

Chronon / Add Clear

Kills Minotaurs, collects Chronons, and runs them through the hoop.

Mechanics

  1. Kill Minotaurs in the middle to create Chronons.
  2. Run Chronons through the hoop until the hourglass fills.
  3. Shoot the Vex node under the hourglass to begin the timing phase.
  4. Pillar players watch the metronome and shoot their assigned pillar on the correct tick.
  5. The runner uses platforms to climb and interact with the objective.
  6. Repeat the build sequence until damage starts.
  7. Group for damage and burn Iatros.
Timing Rule

Pillar shots need to happen together. Count the metronome out loud so all pillar players shoot on the same beat.

3

Epoptes, Lord of Quanta

Epoptes is the Hydra-style encounter. Treat it as a communication-heavy boss fight built around lens/objective calls, Chronon handling, room control, and damage setup.

Epoptes encounter guide

Roles

Callout Team

Reads the active mechanic information and tells the team where to move or shoot.

Chronon Team

Collects and banks Chronons when the encounter requires progression.

Add Clear

Controls Vex pressure and protects players handling the mechanics.

Mechanics

  1. Assign readers/callers before starting the fight.
  2. Clear enemies and watch for objective/lens information.
  3. Use callouts to identify which mechanic location needs attention.
  4. Move Chronons or interact with the required mechanic based on the active call.
  5. Prepare for damage once the boss becomes vulnerable.
  6. Repeat the mechanic cycle until Epoptes is defeated.
Team Tip

Keep callouts short and consistent. Epoptes becomes much easier when one player controls the callout flow.

4

Keregos, the Worldline

Keregos is the final encounter. It brings together Desert Perpetual's Chronon, Temporality, portal, and damage-extension ideas into a final boss fight.

Keregos encounter guide
Keregos damage phase

Roles

Chronon Handlers

Collect and route Chronons to keep the encounter progressing.

Mechanic Callers

Read portal, alignment, or phase information and direct the team.

DPS Team

Prepares burst damage and rotates safely during the damage phase.

Mechanics

  1. Clear enemies and establish safe control of the arena.
  2. Use Chronons and portal mechanics to advance the phase.
  3. Follow caller instructions for alignment or room-specific objectives.
  4. When the damage phase opens, group at the agreed location.
  5. Use heavy weapons, supers, debuffs, and survivability tools together.
  6. Handle the extension or transition mechanic shown in your guide image.
  7. Repeat until final stand and finish Keregos.
Final Boss Rule

Do not let damage phase callouts get buried under DPS chatter. Assign one person to call movement, one person to call timer/extension, and everyone else should keep comms clear.
